Idli, Spice, and Everything Nice

The Mysore Raman Idli Story

By Mukund Sharma | 4 February 2025

In the culturally rich city of Mysore, where the mornings start with the aroma of steaming hot idlis and strong filter coffee, one name has become synonymous with comfort and consistency — Mysore Raman Idli. What began as a street-side stall in the early 90s has now blossomed into a beloved brand cherished not just across the city but throughout Karnataka.

The man behind the magic, Raman Subbiah, was a school dropout with a flair for cooking and an uncompromising spirit. Every day at dawn, he would rise to grind rice and urad dal with his own hands, believing that the secret to soft idlis lay in the love poured into preparation. His wife helped shape and steam them, while Raman served customers personally — always with a warm smile and a sprinkle of chutney powder.

In those early days, his customers were a mix of auto drivers, office workers, and students, all drawn by the consistent fluffiness and mild tang that set his idlis apart. Word spread, and soon, his stall was buzzing by 6 AM every morning. Patrons came not just for the idlis, but for the atmosphere — traditional, humble, and deeply satisfying.

By the mid-2000s, Mysore Raman Idli had become a household name, prompting Raman’s sons to take the legacy to new heights. With the same passion and a bit of business sense, they opened their first sit-down restaurant in 2009. The menu expanded to include spicy chutneys, crunchy vadas, ghee-soaked upmas, and of course, the signature idlis — still steamed fresh in cloth-lined traditional cookers.

What remained unchanged was the dedication to quality. Every outlet sources its ingredients locally, grinds its batter in-house, and follows Raman’s original steaming technique. From Mysore to Bangalore, the brand now runs 12 outlets, and each one tells a piece of the founder’s story — humble beginnings, family spirit, and food that feeds more than just the stomach.

Raman, now retired but still a morning visitor at the flagship store, says with pride, “My idli is my identity. It gave me a life, and now it feeds so many more.” Today, school children grow up with lunchbox idlis from his chain, newlyweds celebrate their first breakfasts there, and even celebrities make secret visits for a quiet, soulful meal.

Mysore Raman Idli is no longer just a restaurant — it’s an emotion rooted in tradition, flavor, and love. As the brand eyes digital delivery, catering, and expansion beyond state lines, one thing is certain: wherever there’s a Mysore Raman Idli, there will be a soft, steamy bite of South Indian soul waiting to be savored.
