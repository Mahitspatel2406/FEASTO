Dosas and Decades of Deliciousness

The Shri Ganesh Bhavan Story

By Shuvra Saha | 11 March 2025

In the vibrant streets of Chennai, where spices fill the air and tradition infuses every bite, one small eatery began a journey that would come to define local taste: Shri Ganesh Bhavan. What began as a humble, two-table roadside restaurant in 1995 is today a proud chain of ten bustling outlets that serve thousands every day. But more than the food, it’s the story of resilience, flavor, and family that truly defines Shri Ganesh Bhavan’s legacy.

Founded by Ganeshan Iyer, a passionate cook and a dreamer from a modest background, Shri Ganesh Bhavan was born out of a single dosa pan and a promise: to never compromise on taste. Armed with his mother’s secret sambar recipe and an undying dedication to hospitality, Ganeshan started cooking dosas, vadas, and idlis for office-goers and students in the Mylapore neighborhood. Word spread, and soon, the queues outside his stall were longer than his operating hours could handle.

“There were days I sold out within two hours,” Ganeshan recalls. “People came not just for the food, but for the feeling of home.” That warm, welcoming spirit remains the brand’s backbone even today.

Over the years, as demand grew, Ganeshan’s sons stepped in with a vision to modernize operations without diluting the authenticity. In 2008, the second outlet opened in T. Nagar, followed quickly by others across Chennai. Yet, the quality never dipped. Every masala dosa is still crisped to perfection, every chutney ground fresh every morning, and every staff member trained to greet with a smile.

Today, the ten outlets serve over 15,000 meals a week, with signature dishes like Butter Ghee Roast Dosa, Filter Coffee, and the ever-popular Mini Tiffin Combo flying out of the kitchen. Yet, the charm of the original outlet remains untouched, preserved like a sacred memory in the heart of Mylapore.

What sets Shri Ganesh Bhavan apart isn’t just the food, but the emotional connect it has built with generations of families. Grandparents bring their grandchildren to show them where they had their first date. Office workers still sneak out at lunchtime for a quick plate of pongal. Even international tourists now list it as a “must-visit” spot in Chennai food guides.

In an era of fast food chains and fleeting trends, Shri Ganesh Bhavan stands as a reminder that success can be built with patience, passion, and a pinch of tradition. The team is now exploring expansion into Bangalore and Coimbatore, but Ganeshan’s philosophy remains unchanged: “We don’t just serve food. We serve memories.”

So next time you bite into a crispy dosa or sip on a piping hot filter coffee from Shri Ganesh Bhavan, remember — you’re not just tasting food. You’re tasting history, love, and a legacy that has been three delicious decades in the making.
