The Big Brand Theory

Spicing up the world, one country at a time

By Sivam Darbar | 4 February 2025

What happens when tradition meets ambition? The story of the iconic South Indian brand Darbar Foods is the perfect answer. Started in a small corner of Tamil Nadu with the dream of making local cuisine global, this legacy brand has now expanded to over five countries, leaving behind a flavorful trail of success and spice.

In the early 2000s, Sivam Darbar and his wife Lakshmi began their journey with a single product — an aromatic, handcrafted sambar masala made using age-old family recipes. The couple sourced each ingredient themselves from the local market, and every blend was ground, roasted, and packed in their small kitchen. Their first customers were neighbors, followed by nearby townsfolk who fell in love with the taste of authenticity.

What truly set Darbar Foods apart was its commitment to quality and culture. The couple wasn’t just selling masalas — they were bottling heritage. Their branding, with traditional motifs and South Indian design, caught attention quickly, especially among NRIs craving a taste of home.

By 2010, Darbar Foods expanded to include ready-to-cook meal kits, chutneys, and pickles. They participated in expos, built a website, and eventually launched in the UAE, Singapore, Malaysia, and the UK — where the South Indian diaspora welcomed them with open arms. Their products became weekend essentials, wedding hampers, and even diplomatic gifts during cultural summits.

Now, the brand boasts a product line of over 80 SKUs, available in 3 languages and compliant with international food safety standards. Yet, the heart of the brand remains in its origin — every spice blend is still curated from Tamil Nadu, and every major decision is run past the founding couple, who now act as brand ambassadors and mentors.

Darbar Foods is no longer just a business; it is a movement — one that blends tradition with innovation, roots with reach. Through decades of dedication and cultural pride, Sivam and Lakshmi have proven that no dream is too spicy to become global.

Their story is an inspiration for every small entrepreneur looking to make a global impact. The Big Brand Theory is not just about products — it's about people, passion, and preserving flavor across borders. What started in one Indian kitchen now finds its place in supermarket aisles around the world.
