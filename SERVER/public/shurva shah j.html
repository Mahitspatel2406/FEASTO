In a world increasingly concerned with climate change and environmental damage, businesses of all types are stepping up to reduce their ecological footprint. The food industry—particularly food delivery—has long been a major contributor to single-use plastic waste. From plastic containers and cutlery to bags and wraps, millions of food orders result in discarded plastics daily. Recognizing this urgent issue, Feasto has launched its Plastic-Free Future Program, an initiative designed to support restaurant partners in adopting more sustainable, eco-friendly packaging solutions.

This blog post dives deep into the program, its goals, how it works, and why it's not just a step forward for Feasto—but for the entire food tech ecosystem.

Why Going Plastic-Free Matters

Plastic pollution has become a global crisis. According to studies, over 300 million tons of plastic are produced each year, with nearly half of it designed for single use. In the food delivery sector, this is a staggering problem. Every takeout container, sauce cup, or plastic bag that isn't recycled contributes to overflowing landfills, polluted oceans, and a future filled with toxic waste.

Feasto recognizes that as a leading food ordering platform, we have a responsibility—not only to serve our users but to serve the planet. That’s why we’ve created a comprehensive, actionable strategy that helps our restaurant partners transition away from plastic, without compromising on food safety or customer satisfaction.

What Is the Plastic-Free Future Program?

Feasto’s Plastic-Free Future Program is an initiative that supports restaurants in moving away from single-use plastics and toward compostable, biodegradable, or reusable alternatives. It’s not a one-size-fits-all policy—it’s a flexible, customizable framework that fits the unique needs of different restaurants, cuisines, and budget levels.

The program is structured around three key pillars:

  1. Education & Awareness
  2. Partnership & Supply Chain Support
  3. Incentives & Recognition

1. Educating and Empowering Restaurant Partners

Feasto offers free resources, training sessions, and webinars to educate restaurant partners about the impact of plastic use, government regulations, and available eco-friendly alternatives. Our partner dashboard includes a dedicated sustainability tab that provides certified eco-supplier lists, cost comparisons, and guides for training staff.

2. Partnering with Green Suppliers

To reduce cost and improve access, Feasto has partnered with eco-friendly suppliers. Restaurants can source compostable containers, biodegradable cutlery, and recyclable packaging materials directly through the Feasto platform with discounted rates and bulk order tools.

3. Incentives and Recognition

Feasto offers recognition badges, financial incentives, and premium search placement to restaurants that meet plastic-free criteria. This not only promotes environmental responsibility but also attracts eco-conscious customers.

Customer Involvement

Customers are part of the change. Features like an opt-out for plastic cutlery and a sustainability meter let customers track their impact. Reviews also include prompts about packaging quality and sustainability.

Success Stories

GreenLeaf Café increased sales after being recognized for sustainability. Spice Route Express reduced costs via supplier partnerships. Bella’s Bites received better customer feedback after switching to eco-friendly containers.

Challenges and Solutions

Feasto helps overcome higher costs, training gaps, and storage issues with continuous support, supplier tools, and feedback systems. Our goal is to make sustainability the default.

The Road Ahead

Future plans include city-specific compliance, zero-plastic delivery kits, carbon offset calculators, and composting partnerships.
